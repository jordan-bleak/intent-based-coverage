The Problem

One price for every shopper doesn't work

Set package protection pricing too high and attach rates drop. Set it too low and you're giving away margin. Test a new price and watch one number go up while another goes down. The problem isn't the price — it's that every shopper is different, and the price has always been the same for all of them.

Price too high

Attach rates plummet. Shoppers skip protection entirely — even the ones who would have bought at the right price.

Price too low

Attach rates look good, but you're leaving revenue on the table for shoppers who would have paid more.

The Priority

Conversation first,
revenue follows

It's tempting to optimize for revenue per visitor — but that's the wrong place to start. The most durable way to grow package protection revenue is to first maximize how many shoppers say yes. Volume is the engine. Efficiency is what you tune on top of it.

When a shopper on the fence converts at a lower price, that's a win. You've grown the base of protected customers. The revenue compounds from there. PPRPV is the efficiency lens — conversation rate is what determines how much there is to optimize in the first place.

The Model

One metric to rule them all

The model optimizes for Package Protection Revenue Per Visit (PPRPV) — the single metric that captures both attach rate and pricing efficiency in one number.

For shoppers likely to buy, the price goes up. For those who need a nudge, the price comes down to remove friction. Every visit is optimized to generate the most protection revenue possible.
